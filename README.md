# Assignment-DeepThought-CultureTech-Ventures-Private-Limited
This repository contains my submission for the DeepThought Business Analytics Internship assignment.  The objective of this assignment was to identify and evaluate Indian manufacturing companies that fit DeepThought’s “Federer” profile — companies that are growth-oriented, technically driven, and focused on building in-house capabilities.


Research Methodology

To identify companies that match the Federer profile, I used a combination of AI-assisted discovery and manual verification.

I began by designing a structured prompt based on the six ICP criteria (manufacturer, India-based, differentiated product, technical decision-maker, growing sector, and growth signals). I used this prompt with AI tools to generate an initial list of potential companies within my selected city and segment.

However, I did not rely on AI outputs directly. In many cases, the AI-generated results were either inaccurate or incomplete. Therefore, I manually verified each company through a detailed research process.

For every shortlisted company, I performed the following checks:

Verified whether the company is actually based in the selected city (not just a sales office)
Confirmed that it operates within the chosen segment
Reviewed the company’s official website to understand its products and manufacturing capabilities
Checked whether the company is a true manufacturer and not a service provider or distributor
Looked for signs of differentiation such as R&D capabilities, certifications, or specialized products
Identified key decision-makers (Founder/CEO/Director) through LinkedIn and analyzed their educational and professional background to assess technical depth
Estimated revenue/turnover using available sources and cross-verified wherever possible
Looked for growth signals such as hiring activity, expansion announcements, or recent updates on their website

In total, I explored and evaluated 100+ companies to arrive at the final list of 25 that best match the Federer profile.

This process involved multiple iterations of filtering, validation, and cross-checking to ensure that the final dataset is accurate, relevant, and aligned with the assignment criteria.

Key Challenge

One of the main challenges was that AI-generated company lists often contained errors — such as misclassified companies (service vs manufacturer), incorrect locations, or outdated information. This required careful manual validation for each company.

Key Learning

This exercise highlighted that while AI is useful for initial discovery, high-quality research requires manual verification, especially when evaluating nuanced criteria like differentiation, founder background, and growth signals.
